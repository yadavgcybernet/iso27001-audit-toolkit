# Audit Evidence Guide — ISO 27001:2022

**Purpose:** This guide tells auditors and ISMS managers exactly what evidence
to collect and what an external certification auditor will look for, clause by
clause and for key Annex A controls.

**Standard:** ISO/IEC 27001:2022
**Applies to:** Stage 1 & Stage 2 certification audits, internal audits, and surveillance audits

---

## How to use this guide

- **Auditors:** Use this as your evidence request list before and during audit fieldwork
- **ISMS Managers:** Use this to prepare evidence packs ahead of a certification audit
- **Column guide:**
  - *Evidence to request* — what documents/records to ask for
  - *What auditors test* — the specific question or check an auditor will perform
  - *Common gaps* — what is most frequently missing in practice

---

## Part 1 — Mandatory clauses (4–10)

### Clause 4 — Context of the Organisation

| Sub-clause | Evidence to Request | What Auditors Test | Common Gaps |
|---|---|---|---|
| 4.1 Understanding the organisation and its context | PESTLE analysis, context register, board minutes referencing context review | Are internal/external issues documented and linked to ISMS risks? | Context document exists but was never reviewed or updated |
| 4.2 Understanding the needs of interested parties | Stakeholder register; list of requirements (legal, contractual, regulatory) | Are all relevant parties identified? Are their requirements captured and acted on? | Legal/regulatory requirements listed but not mapped to controls |
| 4.3 Scope of the ISMS | Scope document (signed, version-controlled) | Is the scope precise — systems, locations, processes? Are exclusions justified? | Scope is too vague ("all IT systems") — auditors require boundaries |
| 4.4 ISMS | Evidence that ISMS processes are established, implemented, and maintained | Is the ISMS actively operating, not just documented? | ISMS documented but no evidence of ongoing operation |

---

### Clause 5 — Leadership

| Sub-clause | Evidence to Request | What Auditors Test | Common Gaps |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5.1 Leadership and commitment | Board/executive meeting minutes referencing IS; signed IS policy | Is top management demonstrably involved, not just rubber-stamping? | IS policy signed but no other evidence of management engagement |
| 5.2 Policy | Information Security Policy (current, signed by CEO/MD) | Is it appropriate to the organisation? Does it include objectives and commitment to improvement? | Policy is generic/boilerplate with no organisation-specific content |
| 5.3 Roles, responsibilities, and authorities | RACI matrix; job descriptions; IS role appointments | Are roles formally assigned? Does the IS function have authority and reporting line to top management? | CISO or IS Manager role undefined or lacking authority |

---

### Clause 6 — Planning

| Sub-clause | Evidence to Request | What Auditors Test | Common Gaps |
|---|---|---|---|
| 6.1.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities | Risk assessment process document | Is there a defined, repeatable methodology? | Risk assessment is ad-hoc with no documented methodology |
| 6.1.2 Information security risk assessment | Completed risk register (signed, dated); asset inventory | Is risk assessment conducted at planned intervals? Are all assets and threats considered? | Risk register not updated after significant changes |
| 6.1.3 Information security risk treatment | Risk Treatment Plan (RTP); SoA | Does the RTP link to the SoA? Are selected controls justified? Is residual risk accepted? | SoA exists but does not reference the RTP |
| 6.2 Information security objectives | IS objectives document; evidence of measurement | Are objectives measurable? Are they reviewed? Do they link to the IS policy? | Objectives are vague ("improve security") with no KPIs |
| 6.3 Planning of changes | Change management records; meeting minutes | Are changes to the ISMS planned systematically? | No formal change management process for ISMS changes |

---

### Clause 7 — Support

| Sub-clause | Evidence to Request | What Auditors Test | Common Gaps |
|---|---|---|---|
| 7.1 Resources | Budget records; headcount; tool licences | Is adequate resource allocated to the ISMS? | No documented resource allocation for IS activities |
| 7.2 Competence | CVs; training records; certification evidence; job descriptions | Are people doing IS work competent? Is competence evaluated? | Training completed but not documented or linked to IS roles |
| 7.3 Awareness | Awareness training logs; completion rates; quiz results | Do all relevant staff understand the IS policy, their role, and consequences of non-compliance? | Awareness training done once at onboarding — not recurring |
| 7.4 Communication | Communication plan; evidence of IS communications sent | Is there a defined internal/external communication plan for IS? | No formal communication plan — ad-hoc only |
| 7.5 Documented information | Document control procedure; version-controlled documents | Are documents properly controlled, versioned, and accessible? Are obsolete docs removed? | Documents exist but no version control or review dates |

---

### Clause 8 — Operation

| Sub-clause | Evidence to Request | What Auditors Test | Common Gaps |
|---|---|---|---|
| 8.1 Operational planning and control | Procedure documents; change records; configuration records | Are IS processes planned and controlled? Is outsourcing managed? | Processes documented but not followed in practice |
| 8.2 Information security risk assessment | Evidence of risk assessment results at planned intervals | Has the risk assessment been re-run at least annually? | Risk assessment not repeated after changes |
| 8.3 Information security risk treatment | Updated RTP; evidence of control implementation | Is treatment progressing against the plan? Are deadlines met? | RTP items overdue with no documented escalation |

---

### Clause 9 — Performance Evaluation

| Sub-clause | Evidence to Request | What Auditors Test | Common Gaps |
|---|---|---|---|
| 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation | KPI reports; metrics dashboards; IS performance reports | Are IS objectives being measured? Are results analysed and used for decisions? | Metrics collected but never reviewed or acted on |
| 9.2.1 Internal audit (General) | Internal audit programme; auditor qualifications/independence evidence | Is there a documented internal audit programme covering the full ISMS? | Internal audits not planned in advance or not covering all clauses |
| 9.2.2 Internal audit programme | Audit schedules; completed audit reports | Have audits been conducted as scheduled? Are findings recorded? | Audit report exists but findings are not followed up |
| 9.3.1 Management review (General) | Management review meeting minutes (dated, signed) | Has top management reviewed the ISMS at planned intervals (at least annually)? | Management review minutes are superficial — no decisions or actions recorded |
| 9.3.2 Management review inputs | Agenda showing all required inputs (audit results, incidents, objectives, etc.) | Were all mandatory review inputs covered? | Review agenda missing required inputs (e.g. supplier performance, incidents) |
| 9.3.3 Management review results | Action log from management review | Were outputs (improvement decisions, resource needs) documented and tracked? | Actions not assigned owners or deadlines |

---

### Clause 10 — Improvement

| Sub-clause | Evidence to Request | What Auditors Test | Common Gaps |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10.1 Continual improvement | Evidence of improvement actions; PDCA cycle records | Is improvement systematic and ongoing, not only reactive? | No proactive improvement — only reactive to audit NCs |
| 10.2 Nonconformity and corrective action | NC register; corrective action records; root cause analyses | Are NCs investigated to root cause? Are corrective actions verified effective? | Corrective actions closed without verifying effectiveness |

## Part 2 — Key Annex A control evidence (selected controls)

These are the controls most frequently tested in detail during certification audits.

### A.5.1 — Policies for information security

| Evidence to Request | Audit Test | Common Gap |
|---|---|---|
| IS Policy and supporting topic-specific policies | Is the policy suite comprehensive, current, and approved by top management? | Policy exists but subsidiary policies (access control, incident, etc.) are missing |

---

### A.5.9 — Inventory of information and other associated assets

| Evidence to Request | Audit Test | Common Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Asset register (information assets, hardware, software, people, services) | Is the register complete, current, and do assets have assigned owners? | Asset register lists hardware only — information assets not included |

---

### A.5.15 & A.5.18 — Access control and access rights

| Evidence to Request | Audit Test | Common Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Access control policy; user provisioning records; access review logs | Is access based on need-to-know? Are reviews conducted at least annually? Is joiner/mover/leaver process documented? | Access rights never reviewed — former staff accounts still active |

---

### A.5.24–A.5.27 — Incident management

| Evidence to Request | Audit Test | Common Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Incident response plan; incident log; post-incident review records | Is there a documented and tested incident response procedure? Are incidents logged and reviewed for lessons learned? | Incidents dealt with informally — no log, no post-incident review |

---

### A.6.3 — Awareness, education and training

| Evidence to Request | Audit Test | Common Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Training records; completion logs; awareness materials; quiz scores | Is training role-specific? Is it recurring (not just at onboarding)? | Annual completion rate low; no records of who completed training |

---

### A.8.5 — Secure authentication

| Evidence to Request | Audit Test | Common Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Authentication policy; MFA configuration screenshots; password policy | Is MFA enforced on critical systems and remote access? | MFA policy exists but not enforced technically |

---

### A.8.7 — Protection against malware

| Evidence to Request | Audit Test | Common Gap |
|---|---|---|
| AV/EDR deployment reports; update logs; scan results | Is endpoint protection deployed on all endpoints and kept current? | Coverage gaps — BYOD or unmanaged devices not covered |

---

### A.8.8 — Management of technical vulnerabilities

| Evidence to Request | Audit Test | Common Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Vulnerability scan reports; patch management records; remediation log | Is vulnerability scanning performed regularly? Are critical patches applied within a defined SLA? | Scans run but critical findings unpatched for months |

---

### A.8.13 — Information backup

| Evidence to Request | Audit Test | Common Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Backup policy; backup logs; restore test records | Are backups performed per policy? Are restores tested at least annually? | Backups run but restores never tested — failures discovered only during incident |

---

### A.8.15 — Logging

| Evidence to Request | Audit Test | Common Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Logging policy; SIEM/log management configuration; retention records | Are logs collected from critical systems? Is retention period defined and enforced? Are logs protected from tampering? | Logs collected but no defined retention, no review, and no tamper protection |

## Part 4 — Common audit findings (what to avoid)

These are the most frequently raised nonconformities in ISO 27001 certification audits:

| Finding | Clause / Control | How to avoid it |
|---|---|---|
| Risk register not updated after infrastructure or business changes | 8.2 | Schedule risk review triggers in your change management process |
| SoA not linked to Risk Treatment Plan | 6.1.3 | Cross-reference each SoA control to the RTP before audit |
| Internal audit not covering all ISMS clauses | 9.2 | Use a rolling multi-year audit plan covering all clauses |
| Management review minutes lacking substance (no decisions, no actions) | 9.3 | Use a structured agenda template; record decisions and owners |
| Corrective actions closed without evidence of effectiveness verification | 10.2 | Require effectiveness check before closure in your NC procedure |
| Awareness training records incomplete or out of date | 6.3, A.6.3 | Automate training reminders; keep LMS export as evidence |
| Asset register incomplete — missing information assets | A.5.9 | Classify information assets (data) separately from hardware |
| Access rights never formally reviewed | A.5.18 | Schedule quarterly access recertification; keep sign-off records |
| Backup restore never tested | A.8.13 | Run and document restore test at least annually |
| MFA not technically enforced despite policy requirement | A.8.5 | Audit technical enforcement, not just policy existence |
